A Life in Theater

By MJ Madigan

Theater, with a lower-case "t", has fueled David Rockwell's drive for the past 20 years. But it's collaboration, the new and the scary, and the movement of not having the answer that keeps the creative engines revved for this unpretentious architect who wears his passions as comfortably as his trademark flannel shirt.

This Rockwell Group's loft on New York's Union Square West has that off-broadway feeling the tiny industrial-grunge lobby is set like a stage with Rockwell across a giant molded wing chalf from Grand Central Station's Food Court, a Victorian red-velvet chaise from Bar Bas and, for high-tech contrast, a wall clock with a lassey-light doggy that ticks off the seconds. Just inside, there's a birch-twig from Mogegan San Castino-or is from Nobors? A tightly layered collage of frabrics, images, grass, beans, flowers, potter, shorter branches, and upheria-stuff of projects past and present-filling every cranny of this humming arches, where thearter begets Theater.
